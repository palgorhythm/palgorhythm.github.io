Fractal Polyrhythm: multiple voices sound at regular intervals, where the rhythmic ratio between adjacent voices is equal.
Taking this ratio to the power of the distance in levels between to nonadjacent voices gives a composite polyrhythm.
For example, in a 3-level 2:3 polyrhythm, we have four voices, and suppose their names are A, B, C, and D.
Some ratios in this setup are B-C: 2:3, A-C: 4:9, and A-D: 8:27.
